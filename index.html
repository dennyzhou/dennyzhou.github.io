DENNY ZHOU

Email: dennyzhou at google dot com

I am a Senior Staff Research Scientist in Google Brain. Prior to joining Google in 2017, I was a Principal Researcher in Microsoft Research. I was in the Machine Learning Group managed by Chris Burges from 2006 to 2016, and then moved to the Deep Learning Group under Li Deng. Before that, I was a Research Scientist of the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, the Empirical Inference Department headed by Bernhard Schölkopf, from 2002 to 2005, followed by a short stay at the Machine Learning Department of NEC Laboratories America (Princeton campus) hosted by Vladimir Vapnik.

RESEARCH INTERESTS

My primary research interest is in machine learning algorithms and applications: (1) Efficient modeling. Designing neural models to efficiently learn from data; (2) Fast training. Developing algorithms to speed up deep learning training; (3) Fast inference. Developing compact neural models to meet extreme memory and latency constraints. I am also interested in developing algorithms toward true natural language understanding.
